## 📝 Product Overview

**Horizontal Carbonized Bamboo Plywood** (also known as industrial bamboo panels or sheets) is a high-performance, eco-luxury surfacing and structural material engineered for premium interior millwork, cabinetry, and architectural joinery. By flat-pressing selected Moso bamboo strips in a unified horizontal orientation, this panel beautifully showcases the traditional, prominent structural bamboo "knuckles" and natural organic nodes across its face.

This series features a 20mm thickness built via a specialized **3-layer structural profile with a high-stability vertical core**. To achieve the elegant **Carbonized (Caramel / Amber Toned) color finish**, the raw bamboo materials are pressure-steamed for 15 minutes, carmelizing the plant's natural sugars to yield a deep, uniform hue throughout the board. Cross-laminated and hydraulic heat-pressed, these unfinished 4'x8' sheets offer a free-formaldehyde emission rating and deliver **3 to 4 times the dimensional stability of traditional Red Oak**.

## 🏭 Manufacturing Process

Our heavy-duty industrial bamboo plywood is engineered using premium Moso bamboo stalks harvested at peak maturity from ecological forestry zones in **Anhui**, Jiangxi, Fujian, and Zhejiang provinces, with our main extraction base nestled within the **Huangshan Mountain** range.

1. **Precision Slicing & Planing:** Raw mature bamboo poles are cut, split, and planed into fine, uniform rectangular strips.
2. **Pot-Steaming Carbonization:** The bamboo strips are placed in a high-pressure treatment pot and steamed for 15 minutes to fully caramelize internal starches, safeguarding the panel from future wood-boring pests while locking in the rich amber color.
3. **Kiln Drying & Sanding:** Strips are kiln-dried to an optimized, uniform moisture content and sanded flat.
4. **Cross-Laminated Stacking:** Coated evenly with premium non-toxic adhesive matrices, the strips are arranged in parallel layers, stacking horizontal faces over a solid vertical core to counteract directional stress.
5. **Heavy Hydraulic Hot-Pressing:** The stacked layers are subjected to immense hydraulic pressure and heat-cured into solid, stable 2440x1220mm sheets.
